Evolution of The Monkees

some of the milestones in the history of the band

1966

The Monkees are formed, coinciding with the launch of their tv series.

1966

This year also sees the release of The Monkees self-titled debut album.

1967

With the enourmous success of The Monkees' tv series, this year sees the release of three albums from the band:

'More Of The Monkees',

'Headquarters', and

'Pisces, Aquarius, Capricorn & Jones Ltd.'.

1968

Two more albums this year from The Monkees ('The Birds, The Bees & the Monkees' and 'Head') puts them on track to becoming one of the biggest bands of the 60's.

Total sales up to this point would give the band's ablums a total of 15 times platinum.

Shortly after the release of 'Head', Peter Tork left the band. It would be 1987 before he would record with The Monkees again...

1969

The albums 'Instant Replay' and 'The Monkees Present' are released this year; the first two not to feature all four original band members.

After the release of the second of this years albums, Michael Nesmith also left the band. He'd not return until 1996.

1970

With only two of the original members left, Micky Dolenz and Davy Jones, The Monkees released 'Changes', the bands 9th studio album.

This would be the bands last studio album for seventeen years!

1976

Due to the continued popularity for The Monkees tv series, a 'The Monkees Greatest Hits' re-enters the charts.

1986

MTV rekindled interest in The Monkees by rerunning the tv series. This lead to a 20th Anniversary reunion tour later that year with Dolenz, Jones, and Tork.

Tv channel Nickelodeon then picked up the torch and began running the original tv series daily. This lead to sales boom of the original Monkees albums, along with the release of a new greatest hits collection, which went platinum.

1987

'Pool It!' is released, the bands 10th studio album, and the first in seventeen years! Only three of the original lineup (Davy, Micky and Peter) reunited for this album. It was followed by a two year worldwide concert tour.

1996

All four of the original Monkees get back together to record 'Justus', the bands 11th studio album. This was followed by extensive concert tours.

2011

The Monkees, minus Michael Nesmith, launched a massive tour of the UK and USA to mark the bands 45th anniversary.

2012

Davy Jones, RIP. Always a daydreamer. Always a believer.

2016

This year saw the release of the bands 12th studio album 'Good Times', to coincide wit hthe bands 50th anniversary.

2018

The band release their first ever Christmas album, titled 'Christmas Party'. It features new material from The Monkees as well as covers of Christmas classics. This is the bands 13th studio ablum to date.
